# Competitive Landscape: "Goodreads for Recipes"

*Last updated: February 2026*

## Market Context

The recipe app market is ~$800M-$6B (2025), growing at 10-13% CAGR. The space fragments into: personal organizers, social platforms, AI-powered import tools, and legacy content sites. Nobody has nailed the full "Goodreads for recipes" model.

## Tier 1: Major Players

### Allrecipes (Dotdash Meredith)
- 40M+ active users. Largest recipe website globally.
- **Model:** Ad-supported content site. User-submitted recipes with star ratings and reviews.
- **Strength:** Canonical recipe model — recipes accumulate community ratings.
- **Weakness:** Feels 2008-era. No personal library, no social graph, no mobile-first experience. SEO-optimized for Google, not for users.

### Cookpad (Japan, public)
- 60M monthly users in Japan, ~40M globally. Market cap ~$81M (down significantly).
- **Model:** Freemium (premium unlocks search, rankings). Revenue ~$37-75M/year.
- **Strength:** Closest to a social recipe network. Community-driven content.
- **Weakness:** Japan-centric, international expansion failed. Leadership changes, layoffs. No fork mechanic, no creator monetization. Dated UI.

### Samsung Food (formerly Whisk, acquired 2019)
- **Model:** Free, subsidized by Samsung for appliance ecosystem.
- **Strength:** AI recipe discovery, meal planning, grocery integration.
- **Weakness:** Utility-first, social is bolted on. Samsung branding limits appeal. No creator economy.

### Yummly — DEAD (Dec 2024)
- Whirlpool laid off entire team (April 2024), shut down permanently (December 2024).
- **Lesson:** Recipe apps subsidized by hardware companies get killed when hardware priorities shift. Recipe apps need their own sustainable business model.

## Tier 2: Social-First Contenders

### Pepper
- ~100K downloads, ~1M community, 50K+ recipes. Positions as "vertical social for cooking."
- **Model:** Free with premium. Claims $1B ambition.
- **Strength:** Social-first with community discovery.
- **Weakness:** Significant quality problems with AI-imported recipes (wrong ingredients, fabricated instructions). Cannot edit recipes after posting. Small user base. Ambition doesn't match execution.

### BigOven
- 200K+ recipes. "Use Up Leftovers" feature.
- **Model:** Freemium (Pro removes ads, unlocks unlimited uploads).
- **Weakness:** Legacy feel. Weak social graph. No creator monetization.

## Tier 3: Personal Organizers (Not Social)

|---|---|---|
| **Paprika** | Robust organizer, cross-platform, one-time purchase | Zero social features, dated UI |
| **Mela** | Beautiful Apple-ecosystem UI, iCloud sync | Apple-only, zero social, no Android/web |

## Tier 4: AI-First Newcomers (2024-2026)

Flavorish, Honeydew, Recipe One, OrganizEat, Recify — all competing on AI recipe extraction from social media. Undifferentiated. None building toward Goodreads model.

## Key Insights

### 1. "Goodreads for Recipes" is an unclaimed position
The pieces exist in different apps but nobody combines: canonical entries + personal shelves + ratings accumulation + social graph + creator monetization.

### 2. The fork mechanic is a genuine differentiator
GitHub-style recipe lineage (save, modify, link back to original) does not exist in any consumer recipe app. Pepper explicitly lacks recipe editing after posting.

### 3. Creator monetization is zero in recipe apps
Creator economy is $29B market growing 20%+ CAGR. Recipe-specific creator monetization inside apps is essentially $0. First mover advantage is available.

### 4. Appliance-company strategy failed
Both Yummly (dead) and Samsung Food (utility tool) show that hardware-subsidized recipe apps don't become social platforms.

### 5. Pepper is the closest competitor but executing poorly
~100K downloads, quality issues, no edit/fork. If they fix execution, they're the threat. But their head start is small.
