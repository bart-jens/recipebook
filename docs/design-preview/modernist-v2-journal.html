Lamb Shoulder with Pomegranate & Mint

3h 30m
Time
4
Servings
5.0
Rating
Slow-roasted until the meat pulls apart effortlessly, then finished with a jewel-bright pomegranate molasses glaze and a scattering of fresh mint. A centerpiece dish that fills the whole house with warmth.
Ingredients
  • Bone-in lamb shoulder 1.8 kg
  • Pomegranate molasses 3 tbsp
  • Fresh mint leaves 1 bunch
  • Garlic cloves, crushed 6
  • Cumin seeds 2 tsp
  • Olive oil 3 tbsp
  • Pomegranate seeds 1/2 cup
  • Flaky sea salt to taste
Method
  1. 01

    Pat the lamb shoulder dry. Score the fat side in a crosshatch pattern. Rub all over with olive oil, crushed garlic, cumin seeds, and a generous amount of salt. Let it come to room temperature for 30 minutes.

  2. 02

    Preheat the oven to 160 C (320 F). Place the lamb in a deep roasting pan, fat side up. Add a splash of water to the bottom. Cover tightly with foil and roast for 3 hours.

  3. 03

    Remove the foil. Brush the top generously with pomegranate molasses. Increase the oven to 200 C (390 F) and roast uncovered for 20 more minutes until deeply caramelized.

  4. 04

    Rest the lamb for 15 minutes. Pull the meat into rough shreds using two forks. Scatter with pomegranate seeds, torn fresh mint, and a final drizzle of pomegranate molasses. Finish with flaky sea salt.
